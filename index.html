Empowering rural and remote communities through integrated development.

BCT works for socio-economic transformation of rural and remote areas of India, especially weaker and socially underprivileged groups, through physical, social, cultural and economic development of rural people, groups and institutions.

About BCT

A legacy of rural development, service and community partnership.

BCT was started in 1962 from the vision of late Shri P. D. Agarwal. The South India Initiative was initiated in 1996 and is headed by the Bangalore office.

Education Programmes

BRJD Public School, Prabhu Dhan Degree College and rural education initiatives.

BCT founded Bhoruram Jiram Das Public School in 1976 to offer formal education to children. Today, BRJD Public School draws more than 1,200 students. Prabhu Dhan Degree College was opened in 2012 to further academic opportunities.

View education work
Health Programmes

Eye camps, hospital services and community health outreach.

Twice every month in Bhorugram, Chogmal Bhoruka Hospital runs eye camps to perform surgeries and improve eyesight for more than 100 individuals per camp, offered free of charge.
